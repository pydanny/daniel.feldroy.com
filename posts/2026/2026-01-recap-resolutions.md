Better late than never! Here's my annual recap!

## Recap of 2025

- We moved from London, UK to [Manila, Philippines](https://daniel.feldroy.com/posts/2026-02-we-moved-to-manila)
- Spent another year with my soul mate, Audrey. Spent time together doing fun things like coding, cooking, traveling, yoga, and raising our daughter together
- [Uma](https://daniel.feldroy.com/tags/uma) spent her 6th year on Planet Earth. She's really growing, spent the year focusing on school, goofy kid play, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, swimming, engineering, and science. Working on science kits and activities with her is one of my greatest joys. I also became a coach at her BJJ school; my blue belt helps validate my role there. She's quite the character and we adore her
- Suffered a serious back injury from some bad weight lifting that had me bedridden for the first two months of the year. What was effective for my recovery included acupuncture, healthy diet, stretching, mobility exercises, living in a warm country, and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ). These days I'm much more careful about partners and restrict my sparring to just 2-3 rolls per session
- Started doing a lot more gi BJJ. London is very much a city of no-gi practitioners, but in Manila most schools focus on gi. It's funny when you think about the uniform preferences of each since, since the gi makes more sense in the cooler weather of London compared to the tropical heat of Manila
- Spent much of the year in the Philippines. I appreciate the people and culture - individuals in general are friendly, hard-working, pragmatic, and trustworthy. Watching the nation improve (reduced poverty, improved infrastructure) since I first visited in 2012 is inspiring
- Left an AI lab and have been focusing on startups and FOSS
- Started the [Air web framework](https://airwebframework.org/) with [Audrey](https://audrey.feldroy.com/)
- [Rewrote this blog in Air](https://github.com/pydanny/daniel.feldroy.com)
- [Spoke at PyCon Davao 2025](https://pycon-davao.durianpy.org/) and various meetups
- Snorkeled for the first time in nearly 20 years. I did it in Mexico back in 2006, and this time in the Philippines in Batangas. It was amazing to see the coral and fish life up close again
