In a day it will have been three months since I was injured.

Except for the speed at which I recover from symptoms, it's as if my recovery has stalled for the past month. The MRI and EKG revealed nothing, which is typical for concussions. Yet I can only function professionally for at most two hours a day. On a stressful day I can only work 10 minutes before I become useless.

I noticed I suffer more frequent insomnia, resulting in mornings where I wake up with symptoms. It's not fun. I actually have insomnia this evening, hence why I'm writing this entry.

Of concern is that I've noticed gaps in my short and long term memory. I've taken to often writing things down. It's not an age thing, it becomes more acute as my symptoms are triggered.

Fiction writing remains my safe pursuit. I can pump out fiction nonstop without any adverse consequences. The challenge is that compared to tech or writing about tech, fiction writing is hard industry to make a living wage in.

Okay, details about what I'm going through:

## Triggers

Slow triggers taking 60-90 minutes to cause symptoms:

- Crowds
- Coding 
- Working with LLMs
- Exercise

Medium triggers that take effect in 10 minutes:

- Learning new things like board games, programming languages, and human languages
- Reviewing easy pull requests
- Stress

Fast triggers that take effect in 1-3 minutes:

- Reviewing complex pull requests
- Very fine print on paper
- Visual distortions like blurry text, blurry photos, spatial puzzles, video recursion
- Keeping my eyes on a point and moving my head around quickly (vestibulo-ocular reflex mismatch)
- Context switching within a task. For example, when working on code if I switch from debugging to correcting a DNS failure

Very fast triggers that take effect in seconds:

- Context switching beween major task categories requiring deep thought

## Symptoms

Constant symptoms:

- Light brain fog
- Memory problems, both short and long term

Periodic Symptoms

- Increased incidents of insomnia

Triggered symptoms:

- Headache (usually in the front right of my head)
- Dizziness (but no loss of balance)
- Straight lines and geometric patterns become very prominent and distracting
- Acute memory gaps, both short and long term

## Symptom recovery time and progress

- At least an hour
- Last month it was 2-3 hours
