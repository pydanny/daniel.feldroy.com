It's been approximately four and a half months since I hit my head. I've gotten better, to the point some days I forget I was hurt earlier this year. Other days I run into challenges.

Regular insomnia continues to be an issue. I sometimes take melatonin. I would take it more often but I have a very high tolerance to the drug. I don't like taking any medication and don't want to build up my tolerance further, as you can overdose on melatonin. For reference, other sleep medications are very problematic for me.

When I run into symptoms (usually in crowds or coding) it starts with a feeling on the right side of my head. Focusing on anything becomes really hard, and I struggle n ot to become moody or belligerant. The personality change has damaged some relationships, I'm hoping that I can mend them in the future.

Fiction writing remains my safe pursuit, and I've built a seperate site at [grimdaniel.com](https://grimdaniel.com/) for that effort. On the plus side, exercise isn't an issue anymore. I spend a lot of time in the gym and swimming.

Here's the detail list:

## Triggers

Very fast triggers that take effect in seconds:

- Context switching beween major task categories requiring deep thought
- Keeping my eyes on a point and moving my head around quickly (vestibulo-ocular reflex mismatch)

Fast triggers that take effect in 5-10 minutes:

- Reviewing pull requests
- Stress
- Context switching within a task. For example, when working on code if I switch from debugging to correcting a DNS failure
- Very fine print on paper
- Visual distortions like blurry text, blurry photos, spatial puzzles, video recursion

Medium triggers taking 60 minutes to cause symptoms:

- Learning new things like board games, programming languages, and human languages
- Coding 
- Working with LLMs

## Symptoms

Periodic Symptoms

- Increased incidents of insomnia

Triggered symptoms:

- Headache (usually in the front right of my head)
- Loss of focus
- Acute memory gaps, both short and long term

## Symptom recovery time and progress

- A few minutes to an hour depending on the severity
- Often the following day I am in a light brain fog
